Discover Alexandria, Egypt

Experience the allure of Alexandria, a coastal city steeped in history and culture on the Mediterranean coast of Egypt. Known as the "Pearl of the Mediterranean," Alexandria captivates visitors with its ancient ruins, majestic landmarks, and vibrant waterfront promenades. From its legendary lighthouse to its bustling souks, Alexandria offers a fascinating blend of past and present.

About Alexandria

Founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BCE, Alexandria quickly became one of the most important cities in the ancient world, renowned for its unparalleled intellectual and cultural achievements. Today, Alexandria retains much of its storied past, with landmarks such as the Library of Alexandria and the Citadel of Qaitbay standing as testaments to its rich heritage. Visitors to Alexandria can explore its historic neighborhoods, including the UNESCO-listed Old Town, where narrow alleyways and medieval architecture evoke a sense of bygone eras. The city's bustling Corniche, a scenic waterfront promenade, offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and is the perfect place to relax and soak in the city's coastal charm.
